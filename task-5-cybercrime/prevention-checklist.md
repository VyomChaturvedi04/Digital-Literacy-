**PREVENTION CHECKLIST**

College life in India is basically lived online—from filling out exam forms on precarious portals to split-billing every *chai* and *samosa* via UPI. But as our digital footprint grows, so does the target on our backs.

---

# 🛡️ The 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist

## 1. The Golden Rule of UPI
**Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** Whether it’s a "refund" for a food delivery or a buyer on an online marketplace, remember: you only need your PIN to **send** money. If someone asks you to scan a QR code to "receive" a payment, it’s a scam.

## 2. Screen Sharing is Not Caring
Never download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or RustDesk at the request of "Customer Care." These apps allow scammers to see your screen in real-time, including your passwords and OTPs. Official banks or tech support will **never** ask for this.

## 3. Verify 'Urgent' Requests
If a friend DMs you on Instagram or WhatsApp asking for an emergency loan because they are "stuck in a hospital," **call them first.** With the rise of deepfakes and hacked accounts, it’s easy for a scammer to impersonate someone you know.

## 4. The "Free Wi-Fi" Trap
Campus Wi-Fi and café internet are great for research, but terrible for banking. Avoid logging into your bank accounts or making UPI payments on public networks. If you must, use a reliable VPN to encrypt your data.

## 5. Spot the 'Internship' Scams
Be wary of WhatsApp messages offering "Part-time jobs" or "Work from home" that pay ₹5,000 a day for liking YouTube videos. If they ask for a "registration fee" or a "security deposit" for a laptop, run. Real companies pay you; you don't pay them.

## 6. Lockdown Your Socials
Set your social media profiles to private. Scammers use "OSINT" (Open-Source Intelligence) to gather details like your birthday, your pet's name, or your college location to guess your passwords or craft convincing phishing attacks.

## 7. Enforce Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Passwords alone are a relic of the past. Enable 2FA on your Gmail, Instagram, and LinkedIn accounts using an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) rather than just SMS, which can be intercepted via SIM swapping.

## 8. Audit Your App Permissions
That "cool" AI photo editor you just downloaded doesn't need access to your contacts or microphone. Regularly check your phone settings and revoke unnecessary permissions for apps that seem a bit too "curious" about your personal data.

---

## 🚨 If Things Go Wrong: Report It!
If you’ve been scammed or harassed online, don't panic and don't stay silent. Speed is your best friend, especially in financial fraud.

* **Immediate Action:** Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at **1930**.
* **Official Portal:** File a formal complaint at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)**.
* **Bank Alert:** If it’s a financial scam, notify your bank immediately to freeze your account or the transaction.
