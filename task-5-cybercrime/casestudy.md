**Case Study**

# The Bengaluru QR Code Scam

**UPI Fraud** refers to the exploitation of the Unified Payments Interface to trick users into authorizing unauthorized debits. A prevalent method is the **"Request Money"** or **"Fake QR"** scam, where victims believe they are *receiving* funds when they are actually *sending* them.

# Step-by-Step
This case involves a real 2023 incident where a professor at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru, was defrauded of **₹63,000** while selling a washing machine on a second-hand marketplace (Source: SSRANA/News Reports).

* **The Contact:** A fraudster posing as a buyer contacted the professor and agreed to the price immediately.
* **The Bait:** The "buyer" sent a QR code via WhatsApp, claiming the professor needed to scan it to "receive" the advance payment.
* **The Deception:** The fraudster stayed on the call, instructing the professor to enter his UPI PIN to "verify" the transaction.
* **The Theft:** By entering the PIN, the professor inadvertently authorized a "Collect Request." Instead of receiving money, **₹63,000** was instantly debited from his account.
* **The Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately switched off their phone and deleted the chat history.

---

# Who is Targeted?
While anyone can be a victim, fraudsters heavily target **online sellers** on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace. They also frequent **first-time digital users** in rural areas and even **educated professionals** who may be distracted or unfamiliar with the technical rule that "receiving money never requires a PIN."

---

# Consequences
* **Immediate Loss:** Irreversible depletion of bank savings within seconds.
* **Low Recovery Rates:** Only about 6% of UPI fraud amounts are successfully recovered due to the speed of "mule account" transfers.
* **Digital Trauma:** A lasting fear of using digital payment systems, often leading to a return to cash-only transactions.
